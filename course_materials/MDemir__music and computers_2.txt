Microphones
• Condenser with backplate & power source
• Operates by sensing capacitance differences
• Air pressure moves diaphragm
• Capacitance differences create voltage
• Sensitive: Thin diaphragm made of nylon foil →
• Also due to electronic mechanism
• Can pick up subtle nuances of an instrument, flat frequency 
response, ideal for studio use

Microphones
• Dynamic mics with moving coil & magnet
• Operates through magnetic induction
• Air pressure moves diaphragm
• Movement in the magnetic field creates voltage
• Durable due to sturdy parts:
• Coil wires made of metal or dense polymer
• Diaphragm made of thicker nylon, to be able to stress coil wires
• No delicate electronics
• Higher frequencies are attenuated since the parts are heavier
• Ideal for on-stage use or for studio environments with loud amplifiers

Microphones – Capsule design
• Pressure microphones
• The diaphragm is exposed on the front. Every sound wave can reach the 
front and move the diaphragm.
• Pressure-gradient microphones
• The diaphragm is exposed on the back and the front equally. The 
microphone senses pressure differences between the two sides. Sounds 
from either side are cancelled since the diaphragm receives equal 
pressure from both front and back. 

Microphones – Capsule design
Huge improvement over pressure-gradient microphones: Delay
The rear side of the diaphragm is equipped with ports that delay the 
sound. Directivity is achieved:
• Rear sounds will be attenuated since it 
will reach either side at the same time
• Frontal sounds will be strengthened 
since the delay will be close to one 
period

• Interacts with digital audio units (digital 
synths, mixers, SFX units, software)

• The pitch, its velocity, its press/release if we use keyboard / pad input

separate devices.

Speakers
• Once the signal reaches the speaker, it flows through the voice coil.
• Due to the magnetic field, the voice coil moves back and forth.
• The cone is attached to the 
coil, moving along.
• The moving cone pressures 
the air, creating sound.
• Note there are no “digital 
speakers”, since air pressure 
is always analog.
